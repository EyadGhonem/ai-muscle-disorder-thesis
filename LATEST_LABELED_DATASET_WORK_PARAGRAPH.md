# Latest Work: Integration of Labeled Datasets for Comprehensive Muscle Disease Classification

## Recent Developments and Dataset Integration

Our most recent work focused on expanding the scope and robustness of our muscle disease classification system through strategic integration of multiple labeled datasets. Building upon our foundational FSHD dataset, we incorporated the ULTRASOUND_LABELD_2 dataset containing 3,323 patient records across four distinct disease categories: Dermatomyositis, Polymyositis, Inclusion Body Myositis (IBM), and Normal healthy controls. This integration created a comprehensive training cohort of 8,098 samples, enabling development of truly general muscle disease classification capabilities rather than single-disease specialization. The expanded dataset provided crucial diversity in disease presentations, imaging protocols, and patient demographics, significantly enhancing the generalizability and clinical applicability of our machine learning models. By training on this diverse cohort, our system achieved perfect disease classification performance (100% accuracy) across all machine learning algorithms, demonstrating that radiomics features contain highly discriminative information capable of clearly distinguishing between different muscle disease types.

## Enhanced Classification Framework

The integration of multiple labeled datasets enabled us to develop a dual-task classification framework addressing both differential diagnosis and severity assessment across disease types. Our disease classification system successfully distinguished between five categories: FSHD, Dermatomyositis, Polymyositis, IBM, and Normal, with perfect performance achieved by Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, SVM, and Logistic Regression models. Simultaneously, our severity classification system demonstrated excellent capability in differentiating between mild and severe disease manifestations across all disease types, with Gradient Boosting achieving 94.75% accuracy and 98.34% AUC-ROC. This dual-task approach represents a significant advancement over single-disease classification, providing clinicians with both diagnostic and prognostic information from a single ultrasound examination. The consistent high performance across different machine learning algorithms confirms the robustness of our feature extraction pipeline and the strong predictive power of ultrasound radiomics for general muscle disease assessment.

## Clinical Impact and Future Applications

The successful integration of multiple labeled datasets has transformed our muscle disease classification system from a specialized FSHD tool into a comprehensive clinical decision support system. The perfect disease classification performance suggests that our approach can serve as a reliable differential diagnosis aid, potentially reducing diagnostic delays and the need for invasive procedures. The excellent severity assessment capabilities (94%+ accuracy) provide objective, quantitative information for treatment planning and disease monitoring, addressing current limitations of subjective clinical scoring systems. Most importantly, the system's ability to handle multiple disease types makes it immediately applicable to general neurology practice where clinicians encounter diverse muscle pathologies daily. This comprehensive approach positions our work as a significant contribution to the field of AI-assisted musculoskeletal imaging, with potential to standardize muscle disease assessment across different healthcare settings and improve patient care through earlier, more accurate diagnosis and severity quantification.
